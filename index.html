Capstone Project Phase - 2

Urban Growth Forecasting and LULC Dynamics in Bangalore using Random Forest Classification and Cellular Automata on Dynamic World Satellite Data
Urban expansion in fast-growing cities like Bangalore presents major challenges for sustainable planning, especially in managing ecological degradation from rapid land use and land cover (LULC) changes. Existing models often rely on fixed LULC labels from older, lower-resolution datasets and require extensive preprocessing. In contrast, Google’s Dynamic World dataset offers near real-time satellite data at 10-meter resolution, with per-pixel probability scores for nine land cover classes—providing richer and more realistic inputs for classification models. This study proposes a two-stage hybrid framework combining Random Forest (RF) classification with Cellular Automata (CA)–based spatial simulation for urban growth forecasting. The RF model, trained on probability bands from Sentinel-2 imagery, generates an early 2025 LULC map with 98.21% accuracy, a Kappa coefficient of 0.9752, and a mean Intersection over Union (IoU) of 0.9004. A CA model then simulates 2026 built-up expansion using spatial neighborhood logic. The system forecasts a 6.21% increase in urban area and 41.8 sq.km of tree cover loss. Backcasting exercises for earlier years produced IoU scores above 0.86, validating the forecasting logic. Implemented on Google Earth Engine and Python, this interpretable, high-resolution framework supports real-time, data-driven decision-making in urban planning and ecological management.

Undergraduate Research Project - 2

Lightweight Speaker-Aware Separation Using ECAPA Embeddings and DCF-Inspired Masking

Target speaker extraction (TSE) is crucial in applications such as personalized voice assistants and speaker-specific transcription, yet existing systems often rely on complex architectures or high-resource requirements. To address this gap, we propose a lightweight, speaker-aware separation framework that combines pretrained ECAPA embeddings with a DCF-inspired masking architecture for effective target speaker extraction at the waveform level. This study introduces a modular system composed of: (1) an ECAPA-TDNN speaker encoder for robust enrollment-based speaker embedding, (2) a speaker-conditioned masking model that fuses the embedding into a convolutional separation network, and (3) an optional TCN-based post-refiner to enhance output quality. We utilize the MiniLibriMix dataset to simulate realistic two-speaker mixtures with added noise, focusing on single-channel, low-sample-rate (8kHz) conditions. Our model is evaluated using key audio separation metrics: SI-SNR, SDR, and MSE. It achieves strong separation performance with SI-SNR exceeding 15 dB and SDR around 12.1 dB. Visualization of waveforms and spectrograms further confirms qualitative improvements. This work supports the development of efficient, speaker-personalized speech enhancement systems in low-resource and real-time scenarios, demonstrating that effective separation can be achieved with a compact and interpretable architecture.
